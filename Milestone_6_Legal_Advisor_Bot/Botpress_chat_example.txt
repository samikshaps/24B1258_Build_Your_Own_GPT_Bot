explain the environment protect act

The Environment Protection Act, 1986 is a major legislation enacted in India to protect the environment and promote sustainable development. It is one of several key environmental laws in the country, alongside the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 and the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974. The Act empowers the government to take measures to improve and protect the environment, and it serves as an umbrella legislation for environmental protection in India.

what are the main features of our constitution

The main features of the Constitution of India are:

It is the supreme law of the land and provides the framework for governance, rights, and duties.
It establishes India as a sovereign, socialist, secular, and democratic republic.
It outlines the structure, powers, and functions of various organs of the government.
It guarantees fundamental rights and duties to citizens.
It includes the Directive Principles of State Policy.
It ensures the separation of powers among the Legislature, Executive, and Judiciary.
It provides for a federal structure with a strong central government.

what can you tell me about Labour and Employment Laws

India has a broad range of labour and employment laws aimed at regulating employment conditions, wages, benefits, and workers' rights. Key Acts include:

Industrial Disputes Act, 1947
Factories Act, 1948
Minimum Wages Act, 1948
Employees Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952
Recently, several Labour Codes have been introduced to consolidate and simplify existing laws:

Code on Wages
Code on Social Security
Industrial Relations Code
Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions Code
These laws collectively govern aspects such as industrial relations, wage regulation, social security, and workplace safety in India.
